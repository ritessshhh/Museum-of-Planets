Fun Fact About Mercury

Mercury is hot, but not too hot for ice!

Fun Fact About Neptune

Neptune is the Smallest of the Gas Giants

Fun Fact About Venus

Venus has a thick layer of clouds made up of sulfuric acid droplets. These clouds reflect sunlight, making Venus one of the brightest objects in our night sky.

Fun Fact About Jupiter

The planet has a large number of moons, with over 80 known moons orbiting it. The four largest moons, known as the Galilean moons, are Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Fun Fact About Uranus

Due to its extreme axial tilt, Uranus experiences highly unusual seasons. Each pole of the planet experiences about 42 Earth years of continuous sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness.

Fun Fact About Saturn

Saturn has the fastest winds in the solar system, with speeds reaching up to 1,800 kilometers per hour (1,100 miles per hour) in its upper atmosphere.

Fun Facts About Black Hole

Time slows down near a black hole.

Fun Fact About Pluto

If Earth was the size of a nickel, Pluto would be about as big as a popcorn kernel.
